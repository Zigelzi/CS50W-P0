EV terminology

Here is the basic terms and acronyms related to electric vehicles.

Vehicle terminology

Term Explanation
ICE Internal Combustion Engine - The "original" engine which is used to propel vehicles. Heat engine which uses diesel, gasoline or other kind of fuel to create motion in vehicles.
BEV Battery Electric Vehicle - Vehicle which is propelled with electric motor which energy is supplied completely by battery in the car. Does not have Internal Combustion Engine at all.
HEV Hybrid Electric vehicle - Vehicle which is mainly powered by internal combustion engine but has electric motor which is used to assist the ICE. Electric motor is powered by battery but can't be charged from the electric grid.
PHEV Plug-In Hybrid Vehicle - Vehicle which has internal combustion engine and electric motor. Electric motor is powered by the battery and can be charger from the electric grid.

Charger terminology

Term Explanation
CHAdeMO DC charging standard used mainly by Nissan and Tesla Model S/X (adapter) EV:s.
CCS DC charging standard used mainly by European car manufacturers like Audi, BMW and Mercedes-Benz EV:s.
